Details

Structural design is the foundation of payload and satellite development, ensuring mechanical integrity, durability, and optimal performance in extreme conditions. This domain focuses on:

Payload Enclosure Design –Creating outer structures to protect internal components from mechanical stress, vibrations, and thermal fluctuations.

Material Selection –Choosing lightweight, high-strength materials like aluminum alloys, carbon fiber composites, and aerospace-grade polymers for durability and weight efficiency.

Thermal & Stress Analysis –Ensuring structural resilience through simulations and testing against launch loads, radiation, and space vacuum.

Precision Manufacturing –Implementing CNC machining, 3D printing, and advanced fabrication techniques for compact, optimized, and robust structures.

From CubeSats to payload housings, structural design ensures mission success by protecting critical electronics and instruments in extreme environments!

Pre requisites:

  • Basics of physics and mechanics
  • Material properties: Stress strain, Yield Strength, Stiffness, Ductility

BeliefSat-0 Payload Structure

Built with Aluminum 8011, the BeliefSat-0 payload features PEEK thermal insulation and is secured with six M5 HEX steel bolts (torqued to 3.6 N-m). It has a total thickness of 14.35 mm and a mass of 691.1 g. Its moment of inertia and center of mass data serve as the foundation for structural and dynamic analysis.
